## 2. Competitor Landscape

### UK Brand/Identity Agencies Advertising on Meta

Common approaches from competitors:

1. **Lead magnet offers** - Free brand assessment, brand health check, brand audit
2. **Case study leads** - "See how we helped X achieve Y"
3. **Educational content** - "5 signs your brand needs an update"
4. **Before/after showcases** - Visual brand transformations
5. **Webinar/workshop funnels** - Free training as top-of-funnel

### Competitor Ad Formats

| Format | Usage | Performance |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Single image with strong headline | Most common | Consistent |
| Carousel (case study) | Common for agencies | Good engagement |
| Short video (15-30s) | Growing | Highest engagement |
| Testimonial video | Emerging | High trust |
| UGC-style content | Rare for agencies | Untapped opportunity |

### Competitor Landing Pages

Most successful B2B agency landing pages share:
- Headline matches ad promise exactly
- Form visible above the fold (or within first scroll)
- 3-5 sections maximum
- No navigation links (dedicated LP)
- Pricing transparency (at least "from" prices)
- Case study results visible (not behind clicks)
- Trust bar with client logos near form

## 3. Audience Strategy Context

### B2B Decision Makers on Meta

Meta is not the primary B2B platform (LinkedIn is), but it reaches decision makers in leisure mode. Key considerations:

- **Advantage:** Lower CPMs than LinkedIn (GBP 15-20 vs GBP 50-100)
- **Advantage:** Higher engagement rates (people scroll more casually)
- **Disadvantage:** Harder to target by job title/company
- **Disadvantage:** Lower commercial intent (awareness, not search)

### Recommended Audience Strategy for Brand Services

| Tier | Audience | Est. Size | Expected CPL |
|------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| 1 (warmest) | Website visitors 30 days | 500-2,000 | GBP 5-15 |
| 2 | Page/IG engagers 90 days | 1,000-5,000 | GBP 10-25 |
| 3 | 1% Lookalike of converters | 400,000-500,000 | GBP 15-35 |
| 4 | Interest-based (business owners) | 2M-5M | GBP 20-50 |
| 5 | Broad (current approach) | 42M-50M | GBP 50-150+ |

Current approach targets Tier 5 exclusively. Should prioritise Tier 1-3.

## 4. Budget Context

### GBP 15-20/Day Budget Analysis

At GBP 15/day with a GBP 16.70 CPM:
- ~900 impressions/day
- ~500 unique reach/day
- Need 5-7% conversion rate to get 1 lead/day
- Typical landing page CVR is 2-5%

**Mathematical reality:** At current conversion rate (0%), no budget increase will help. Must fix landing page first. Even at 3% CVR with current traffic quality, would get 0.5 leads/day (15/month) at ~GBP 30 CPL.

### Recommended Budget Allocation

| Phase | Budget | Duration | Goal |
|-------|--------|----------|------|
| 1: Fix LP + tracking | GBP 0 (pause ads) | 2-3 days | Implement changes |
| 2: Restart with Contact event | GBP 15/day | 7 days | Exit learning phase |
| 3: Scale if working | GBP 20-25/day | Ongoing | Optimise CPL |

## 5. Platform Considerations

### Why Meta Can Work for Brand Services

1. Visual platform suits visual agency work (portfolio, before/after)
2. Carousel format naturally tells brand transformation stories
3. Decision makers use Meta/IG daily for 30+ minutes
4. Retargeting is powerful (show different angles to warmed visitors)
5. Cost per impression is 3-5x lower than LinkedIn

### Why Meta is Challenging for Brand Services

1. Low commercial intent (awareness mode, not purchase mode)
2. Limited B2B targeting (no job title targeting like LinkedIn)
3. Algorithm needs conversion volume to optimise (50+/week)
4. Longer sales cycles mean delayed ROAS visibility
5. Brand services are high-consideration purchases (not impulse)

### Recommendation

Meta is viable for Milktree with these conditions:
- Dedicated landing page (not full website)
- Low-friction conversion event (email capture, not booking)
- Retargeting emphasis (warm > cold)
- Nurture sequence post-capture (email drip before sales call)
